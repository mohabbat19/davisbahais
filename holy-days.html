General Principles for Holy Day Observances

Spiritual Focus

The primary purpose is spiritual celebration and commemoration. Programs should create a reverent atmosphere conducive to prayer, reflection, and connection with the significance of the Holy Day.

Community Building

Holy Days unite the community across generations. Provide meaningful roles for children, youth, and adults. Create opportunities for all community members to contribute their wisdom and experience.

Welcoming Environment

These gatherings should warmly welcome both Bah√°'√≠s and friends of the Faith. Create an inclusive atmosphere where newcomers feel comfortable and can understand the significance of the occasion.

Thorough Preparation

Begin planning at least 6 weeks in advance. Conduct regular consultations, delegate responsibilities clearly, and confirm all arrangements well before the event.

Effective Communication

Announce Holy Days at least 3 to 6 weeks in advance through multiple channels: email, social media, community gatherings, and personal invitations. Coordinate with Children's classes and Junior Youth groups to present, perform, or contribute to the program. Build anticipation through reminder messages.

Cultural Sensitivity

Davis's diverse community enriches our celebrations. Welcome artistic expressions and cultural traditions that align with the spirit of the Faith and the Holy Day being commemorated.

Feedback and Continuous Improvement

Build feedback mechanisms into all event preparations. After each Holy Day, gather input from participants and the task force. Share lessons learned with the LSA and future task forces to continuously improve our celebrations.

Financial Support

The LSA can make funds available for these important gatherings. Submit budget requests to the LSA at least 2 to 4 weeks in advance. Depending on the occasion, light refreshments may suffice, other times a potluck is more reasonable, and yet other times, such as the Birth of Bah√°'u'll√°h, a catered meal may be appropriate.

Task Force Planning Template

This comprehensive checklist guides task forces through all phases of Holy Day preparation. Adapt as needed for the specific Holy Day and circumstances.

Phase 1: Initial Planning (6-8 Weeks Before)

  • Receive assignment from LSA and review this guidelines document
  • Schedule first task force meeting within one week
  • Consult on the vision and goals for the celebration
  • Review Davis-specific considerations for this Holy Day
  • Identify who should be invited (Bah√°'√≠s, friends, specific groups)
  • Plan appropriate refreshments (light refreshments, potluck, or catered meal)
  • Prepare budget estimate and submit request to LSA at least 2 to 4 weeks before event

Phase 2: Program Development (5-6 Weeks Before)

  • Design the program structure (devotional, educational, social components)
  • Select readings, prayers, and music appropriate to the Holy Day
  • Identify and invite speakers or presenters
  • Coordinate with Children's classes and Junior Youth groups for presentations or contributions
  • Plan activities for children if families will attend
  • Determine roles for community members (youth, families, elders)
  • Establish feedback mechanism for gathering input during and after the event
  • Create a detailed timeline for the event

Phase 3: Logistics (4-5 Weeks Before)

  • Reserve venue (see Davis Community Venue Guide)
  • Confirm venue has needed equipment (sound system, seating, tables)
  • Plan refreshments and coordinate food contributions or catering
  • Finalize budget and confirm LSA approval
  • Assign specific responsibilities to task force members
  • Create an invitation list with contact information
  • Arrange for setup and cleanup volunteers

Phase 4: Communication (3-6 Weeks Before)

  • Send initial invitation via email to community (3 to 6 weeks in advance)
  • Post announcement on community social media platforms
  • Make personal invitations to friends and seekers
  • Announce at devotional gatherings and study circles
  • Coordinate with Children's classes and Junior Youth groups for their involvement
  • For Twin Holy Days: intensify outreach to UC Davis contacts and wider community
  • Create event flyers or digital graphics if appropriate

Phase 5: Final Preparations (1-2 Weeks Before)

  • Send reminder messages to invited guests
  • Confirm all participants (readers, musicians, speakers, children's presenters)
  • Finalize program order and print copies if needed
  • Purchase or arrange for food and supplies
  • Prepare materials for children's activities
  • Create name tags or welcome materials for newcomers
  • Hold final task force meeting to review all details

Phase 6: Event Day

  • Arrive early to set up venue (at least 30 minutes before, events with decorations and activity stations will require at least an hour)
  • Test sound equipment and arrange seating
  • Set up refreshment area and children's activity space
  • Brief all participants on their roles and timing
  • Assign greeters to welcome guests warmly
  • Ensure someone is designated to welcome and orient newcomers
  • Facilitate the program according to plan
  • Coordinate cleanup and venue restoration

Phase 7: Follow-Up (Within 1 Week After)

  • Send thank you messages to participants and volunteers
  • Follow up with newcomers who attended
  • Gather feedback from attendees (informal/anonymous survey)
  • Hold task force debrief to discuss what went well and areas for improvement
  • Submit budget report and receipts to LSA for reimbursement
  • Document lessons learned for future task forces

Budget Submission Process

To request funds for Holy Day celebrations:

  1. Submit budget request to LSA Secretary 2-4 weeks before the event
  2. Include itemized list: venue costs, food/refreshments, materials, decorations
  3. Note whether refreshments will be light, potluck, or catered
  4. Wait for LSA approval before making purchases
  5. Keep all receipts for submission after the event
