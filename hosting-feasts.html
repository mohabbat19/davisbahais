Your Role as Feast Host
‚ñº

As the Feast host, your primary responsibility is to prepare the devotional portion and provide hospitality during the social portion of Feast. The Local Spiritual Assembly handles all administrative matters during the administrative portion.

‚úì What You ARE Responsible For:

  • Devotional Portion: Select and arrange prayers, readings, and music
  • Music and Arts: Coordinate musical or artistic elements (encouraged)
  • Children's Activities: Prepare engaging activities for children (optional but appreciated)
  • Refreshments: Provide refreshments for the social portion
  • Space Setup: Arrange the meeting space in a welcoming manner
  • Space Cleanup: Restore the space after Feast concludes

Assembly Handles:

  • All aspects of the Administrative Portion
  • Assembly announcements and communications
  • Financial report
  • Reading the National Feast Letter
  • Facilitating consultation
  • Community coordination and follow-up

Important:

You do not need to prepare anything for the administrative portion. Your focus is on creating a beautiful devotional experience and warm hospitality.

Quick Start Host Checklist
‚ñº

One Week Before Feast:

  • Confirm date, time, and location with Local Spiritual Assembly
  • Note: The National Spiritual Assembly sends the Feast Letter directly to all Bah√°'√≠s
  • Review the consultation theme (optional - LSA handles this)
  • Select prayers and readings for devotional portion
  • Arrange for music/arts if desired (coordinate with musicians ahead)
  • Plan children's activities if you choose to include them
  • Plan refreshments (can be as simple as water - spirit matters most!)
  • Recruit helpers if needed for setup, children, or cleanup

Day of Feast:

  • Arrive 30-45 minutes early for setup
  • Arrange seating in circle or semi-circle
  • Create devotional focal point (candle, flowers, prayer books)
  • Prepare refreshments and serving area
  • Set up children's area if applicable
  • Confirm music/readings are coordinated to minimize interruptions
  • Welcome participants warmly as they arrive

After Feast:

  • Thank participants and volunteers
  • Clean up space completely
  • Wash dishes and store leftovers
  • Return space to original condition
  • Return any borrowed items
The Devotional Portion: Creating Sacred Space
‚ñº

The Spiritual Purpose

The devotional portion is the spiritual heart of the Feast. It is a time when the community gathers to immerse itself in the Word of God, allowing divine verses to wash over hearts and souls. This sacred time creates an uplifting atmosphere that:

  • Connects individual hearts to the Word of God
  • Unifies the community in worship and praise
  • Prepares hearts for meaningful consultation
  • Renews spiritual commitment and vision
  • Creates a haven of peace and tranquility
  • Reminds us of our spiritual identity and purpose

üí° Creating the Atmosphere:

The devotional atmosphere should be reverent yet warm, uplifting yet intimate. Your role is to create conditions where hearts can open to the transformative power of sacred scripture. Every element from the physical space to the selection of readings to the quality of music contributes to this spiritual experience.

  • Coloring station: Bah√°'√≠-themed coloring pages related to the month

  • Craft project: Simple craft related to Bah√°'√≠ teachings or month's theme

The Social Portion: Refreshments and Fellowship
‚ñº

The Spiritual Purpose of Hospitality

The social portion allows bonds of friendship to deepen. Refreshments are an expression of hospitality and love, not a requirement for elaborate food.

üíõ Most Important Principle:

Refreshments can be as simple as water. What truly matters is the spirit of fellowship and the warmth of hospitality. The quality of human connection is infinitely more important than the quality or quantity of food.

Refreshment Guidelines

  • Water is sufficient: Clean water served with love is a perfect offering
  • Simple is beautiful: Light refreshments are completely appropriate
  • Cultural sharing: Foods from diverse traditions represented in community
  • Potluck option: Community members can bring items to share the responsibility
  • Timing: Have refreshments ready as administrative portion ends

Financial Consideration:

Please do not feel pressure to spend money on refreshments. If finances are a concern, water alone is perfectly acceptable and honorable. The community values your service, not expensive food. Consider asking if others would like to contribute items (potluck style) if that would help. Also, the LSA can provide funds for refreshments if needed.

Creating Fellowship

During the social time portion (all optional):

  • Circulate to welcome everyone
  • Introduce people who may not know each other
  • Make special effort to include newcomers
  • Keep conversations positive and inclusive
  • Encourage organic connection without forcing activities
  • Organize an activity that helps create a feeling of fellowship
Clean Up and Closing
‚ñº

Clean Up Checklist

  • Clear all dishes, cups, utensils
  • Pack up leftover food
  • Wash dishes or arrange for helpers to assist
  • Wipe down all surfaces
  • Take out trash and recycling
  • Return furniture to original arrangement
  • Collect borrowed items
  • Ensure space is completely clean and ready for next use

üí° Asking for Help

You don't need to do everything alone! Community members are usually happy to help. It's completely appropriate to:

  • Ask someone to help with dishes during social portion
  • Recruit youth to help with setup or children's activities
  • Organize a cleanup team before Feast ends
  • Request potluck contributions to share responsibility
  • Have others bring specific items (ice, cups, napkins)
Frequently Asked Questions
‚ñº
What if I'm hosting for the first time and feel nervous?

This is completely normal! Start simple and remember the community appreciates your service. Focus on creating a warm atmosphere‚Äîperfection is not expected. Ask the Assembly for support, and don't hesitate to recruit helpers. Your sincere effort is what matters most.

How much should I spend on refreshments?

You should spend whatever feels comfortable for you, which may be nothing at all. Water served with love is sufficient and honorable. Remember: the spirit of hospitality matters infinitely more than elaborate food. Never go into debt or financial strain to host Feast.

The administrative portion of regular Feasts is for Bah√°'√≠s only, as it includes community business and consultation. However, the Davis Bah√°'√≠ Community holds Unity Feasts periodically, which do not include the administrative portion and are open to friends of the Faith. What if very few people attend?

A small Feast is still spiritually meaningful! Don't take low attendance personally‚Äîmany factors affect turnout. The spiritual significance of Feast doesn't depend on numbers. Adjust as needed and appreciate the intimate gathering.

Should I coordinate with the Assembly about the theme?

The Assembly handles all aspects of the administrative portion, including the consultation theme. You don't need to coordinate with them about this unless you're curious and want to align your prayer selection with the theme.

What if I need to cancel at the last minute?

Contact the Local Spiritual Assembly immediately. They will help find an alternative host or location. Life emergencies happen‚Äîthe community will understand.

Can I host Feast at a park or outdoor location?

Yes, if weather permits! Outdoor Feasts can be beautiful. Ensure there's adequate seating, shade if needed, and a backup plan for bad weather. Coordinate with the Assembly about logistics.

How do I handle children who are being disruptive during the devotional?

Parents are responsible for their own children. Most will handle disruptions naturally. If needed, having a separate children's area with activities allows parents to step away briefly. Gently explain that the devotional portion is specifically for Bah√°'√≠ sacred scripture. Suggest they might share their thoughts during the social portion or submit them for consideration at another time.

Do I need to read the National Feast Letter?

No. The National Spiritual Assembly sends the Feast Letter to all Bah√°'√≠s, and it will be handled by the Local Spiritual Assembly during the Administrative portion. This is not part of your hosting responsibilities.
